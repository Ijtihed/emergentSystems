Origin

Brian Silverman, 1987. A 4-state cellular automaton that is Turing complete. You draw circuit diagrams pixel by pixel and watch electrons flow through them. A full Wireworld computer has been built within this system.

How It Works

Four states, empty, wire, electron head, electron tail. Heads become tails, tails become wire, wire becomes a head if exactly 1 or 2 neighbors are heads, empty stays empty. That is all, and it supports logic gates, clocks, diodes, and registers.

Applications

Educational tool for digital logic and circuit design. Demonstrates Turing completeness from minimal rules. The most satisfying draw-then-simulate loop of any CA, logic gates, clocks, and signal routing emerge from 4 simple states.
