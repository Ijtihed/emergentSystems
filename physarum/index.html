Physarum Transport Networks info

Origin

Based on the real slime mold Physarum polycephalum, which solves shortest-path and network optimization problems without any central brain. The organism grows transport networks between food sources that rival human-engineered highway systems.

Jeff Jones, "Characteristics of Pattern Formation and Evolution in Approximations of Physarum Transport Networks" — 2010, UWE Bristol. Popularized as generative art by Sage Jensen.

How It Works

Thousands of virtual particles each follow three simple rules: sense the chemical trail ahead at three angles (left, center, right), turn toward the strongest signal, then move forward and deposit more trail. The trail diffuses and slowly decays. This positive feedback loop — deposit, sense, follow — creates self-reinforcing organic networks from pure local interactions.

Applications

Network design and logistics optimization, bio-inspired computing, procedural art generation. Physarum-based algorithms have been used to model Tokyo's rail network, optimize supply chains, and explore maze-solving without any global pathfinding.

How To Use

Click to place food sources — agents will route toward them, forming organic network structures. Drag to paint continuous food trails. Right-click to clear an area. Adjust sensor angle, distance, and decay to change the emergent behavior. Hit random for a fresh configuration.
