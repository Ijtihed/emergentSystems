← back Physarum Transport Networks

Origin

Jeff Jones, UWE Bristol, 2010. Agent-based model of slime mold Physarum polycephalum, which solves network optimization without any central brain. Popularized as generative art by Sage Jensen.

How It Works

12,000 particles each sense chemical trail at three angles, turn toward the strongest signal, move forward, and deposit more trail. The trail diffuses and decays. This feedback loop creates self-reinforcing organic transport networks.

Applications

Bio-inspired network optimization and logistics. Physarum algorithms have replicated Tokyo rail networks. Used in procedural art, maze solving, and supply chain design, all from purely local interactions.

How To Use

Click to place food sources, agents will route toward them. Drag to paint continuous trails. Right-click to clear areas. Tweak sensor angle, distance, and decay to change the emergent behavior.
