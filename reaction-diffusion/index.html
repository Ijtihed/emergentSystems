Origin

Alan Turing proposed reaction-diffusion in The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis, 1952. The Gray-Scott model implemented here was formulated by Gray and Scott in 1983. The surface deformation feature is inspired by 2024 research on curvature-driven pattern dynamics.

How It Works

Implements the standard Gray-Scott equations. Chemical U feeds in at rate f, is consumed by autocatalytic reaction U + 2V to 3V, while V decays at rate f+k. U diffuses twice as fast as V. This imbalance in diffusion rates drives spontaneous pattern formation from uniform initial conditions.

Applications

Explains biological pattern formation, leopard spots, zebrafish stripes, coral branching, fingerprint ridges. Used in procedural textures for games and film, material science, and computational morphogenesis research.
