← back Reaction-Diffusion

Origin

Alan Turing, "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis" (1952) — his final major work. Gray-Scott two-chemical model by Gray & Scott (1983). Recent work (2024-2025) shows curvature-driven dynamics on surfaces.

How It Works

Two virtual chemicals diffuse at different rates and react. The slower one sustains local concentrations, spontaneously forming spots, stripes, spirals, and waves. Feed rate controls replenishment; kill rate controls decay.

Applications

Explains leopard spots, zebrafish stripes, coral branching, and fingerprint ridges. Used in procedural textures for games/film, material science, and computational morphogenesis research.

How To Use

Drag to deform the surface (warps diffusion rates). Shift-click to seed new chemical. Right-click to inhibit. Choose a preset for different pattern types.
