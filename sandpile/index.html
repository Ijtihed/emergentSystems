Origin

Bak, Tang, and Wiesenfeld, 1987. The canonical model of self-organized criticality. Connected to Markov processes, combinatorics, algebraic geometry, tropical geometry, and the Tutte polynomial. Reed College portal.

How It Works

Drop grains of sand on pixels. Any pixel with 4 or more grains topples, sending 1 grain to each cardinal neighbor. The order of toppling does not matter, that is what makes it abelian. Dropping millions of grains at the origin produces fractal mandala patterns with self-similar structure at every zoom level.

Applications

Models self-organized criticality in physics, neural avalanches in neuroscience, earthquake cascades in seismology. The fractal identity element has deep connections to algebraic geometry and chip-firing games on graphs.
