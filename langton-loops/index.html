Origin

Christopher Langton, Self-reproduction in cellular automata, 1984. 8-state CA where loop-shaped organisms clone themselves. Variants include Byl loops (6 states) and Sayama's evolving loops (1999) that mutate during replication.

How It Works

8 cell states with 29 transition rules based on the von Neumann neighborhood. A loop extends an arm, copies its instruction sequence into the arm, the arm detaches and becomes a new loop. The new loop immediately starts cloning itself, filling the grid with replicating organisms.

Applications

Foundational work in artificial self-replication. Demonstrates that self-reproduction can emerge from simple local rules without a central controller. Connects to von Neumann's universal constructor theory.
