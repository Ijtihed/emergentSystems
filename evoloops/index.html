Origin

Sayama, 1999, reviewed in arXiv 2402.03961, 2024. First cellular automata exhibiting true Darwinian evolution of self-reproducing organisms. Loops replicate on a grid, collisions cause mutations. Mutant loops that replicate faster outcompete slower ones.

How It Works

8-state CA like Langton's Loops but with modified transition rules that allow mutations during replication errors. When copying arms collide, the resulting offspring may have different instruction sequences. Natural selection operates without any concept of individual or fitness in the rules.

Applications

Deterministic Darwinian evolution in a CA. Studies open-ended evolution, replicator competition, and the emergence of complexity from simple local rules.
