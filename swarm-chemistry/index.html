Origin

Hiroki Sayama, Swarm Chemistry, Artificial Life 15(1), 2009. Won ISAL Outstanding Paper of the Decade award. Sayama's page.

How It Works

Multiple species of Boids-like particles, each with its own separation, alignment, cohesion, max speed, and perception radius. Particles interact across species, producing orbiting rings, pulsing blobs, and cell-like membranes. Breed favorites to discover new lifeforms.

Applications

Artificial life and morphogenesis research. Interactive evolutionary computation for discovering self-organizing swarm patterns.

Random generates new species parameters. Breed mixes two random species into a new one. Click to attract particles.
Species count (2 to 5, default 3). Buttons breed, random, reset. Friction slider.
